Tai Ji Quan and Qi Gong

Tai Ji Quan, literally translated to “superior extreme boxing”, is also called Tai Chi Chuan by English speakers. It is featured with slow, graceful and dance-like movements from a general audience's point of view.

However, there is much more to it. As all internal breathing exercise known as "qi gong" or "chi gong" would, the real emphasize is on the coordination and integration of mind, body, breathing and movements. It is calm while moving, and it moves with calmness. In other words, Tai Ji Quan is one type of moving Qi Gong.

Researchers proved with modern techniques that there were many benefits of Tai Ji Quan. Not only it retrains the brain so it prevents fall for elderlies or people's with Parkinson's disease, but also does it improve the function of the heart. Moreover, some hospitals in the United States integrate Tai Ji Quan, along with acupuncture and Chinese herbs into their oncology department on top of regular western treatment such as radiotherapy and chemotherapy.

At Harmony Acupuncture & TCM, we also recommend Tai Ji Quan to patients with various conditions. This way, some patients can eventually help themselves once they grasp the true essense of Tai Ji.
